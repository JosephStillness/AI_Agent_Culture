Knowledge Base

The chatbot is built from your assignment idea.

Global Guide answers from this project-specific logic: real cultural pains, community-based value, and responsible use of AI inside a wider student support business.

Task 1

Business Model Canvas

Customer segments include international students, domestic students wanting cross-cultural connection, and new postgraduate students. Key resources are the chatbot, student knowledge, community trust, and MQ partner networks.

Task 2

Value Proposition Canvas

The core pains are loneliness, awkward cultural situations, fear of offending others, and isolation. The gains are belonging, confidence, practical cultural understanding, and a cross-cultural network.

Task 3

Cynefin Domain

The domain is complex because cultural meaning is unpredictable, context-dependent, and evolving. The business must probe, sense, and respond through community feedback.

Task 4

Socio-technical System

People, process, technology, data, and governance must work together. The chatbot supports interaction, but moderators, student stories, privacy rules, and bias review create trust.

Task 5

Responsible AI

Relevant risks are cultural bias, unfair stereotypes, misinformation, offensive content, overconfident advice, and privacy exposure from sensitive student experiences.

Conclusion

Limitations and Future

The service depends on student engagement, good moderation, and safe data use. Future versions can add alumni mentoring, official MQ referrals, multilingual support, and governance dashboards.
