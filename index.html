Reduces confusion and isolation through AI guidance, real student stories, and community events.

Channels

Digital plus campus touchpoints

Chatbot, mobile-friendly hub, social media, MQ clubs, orientation programs, and peer-led events.

Students return to ask questions, share experiences, join events, and build trusted campus networks.

Task 3 · Cynefin

Business domain: Complex

Cultural belonging is not predictable enough for a simple rulebook. The right response depends on context, identity, language, relationship history, and changing student norms.

Why complex

Patterns emerge through interaction

The hub must sense what students experience, learn from stories, and adapt the service over time.

Not obvious

No universal cultural rule

The same behaviour can mean different things across cultures, relationships, and campus situations.

Management response

Probe, sense, respond

Run small events, review chatbot logs safely, monitor feedback, and improve guidance with community input.

Task 4 · Socio-technical Analysis

The chatbot only works when the people, process, data, and governance fit together.

People

Diverse students and moderators

Students ask questions and share experiences; moderators protect inclusion and avoid harmful generalisations.

Process

Ask, share, review, connect

Questions become guidance, useful stories become searchable content, and repeated issues inform events.

Technology

AI guide plus community platform

The chatbot supports sensemaking while the platform hosts stories, event discovery, and feedback loops.

Data

Moderated experiential knowledge

Anonymous questions, tagged stories, event feedback, and flagged responses improve service quality.

Governance

Rules for trust

Content standards, privacy controls, escalation paths, and bias reviews keep the service accountable.

Task 5 · Responsible AI

Relevant principles for a culture-focused AI service

Bias and fairness

Responses must avoid cultural stereotypes and represent multiple perspectives instead of treating one view as normal.

Misinformation control

User-generated stories are labelled as lived experience, moderated, and not treated as official cultural facts.

Harm prevention

Offensive, discriminatory, or unsafe advice is flagged, removed, and escalated to trained human support where needed.

Transparency

The chatbot states its limits clearly: it supports reflection and connection, but it is not an expert authority.

Privacy

The hub should minimise personal data, anonymise sensitive stories, and avoid exposing identity-based vulnerabilities.

Conclusion

Strong potential, with realistic limits.

Limitations

The model depends on student engagement, careful moderation, reliable privacy practice, and the chatbot's ability to avoid overconfident cultural advice.

Future direction

A mature version can expand into alumni mentoring, official MQ support referrals, multilingual content, and stronger governance dashboards for bias and harm monitoring.
